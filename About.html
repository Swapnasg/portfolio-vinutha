From Commerce Classrooms to Creative Studios

Once crunching numbers, now crafting experiences — fueled by coffee, curiosity, and color palettes.

I’ve always been that person who could spend hours doodling in the corner of a notebook or getting lost in the details of a painting. Colors, shapes, and ideas have always fascinated me — I love exploring how creativity can transform something ordinary into something meaningful. Curiosity has always been my compass; whether it’s learning a new design trend, a random art technique, or simply understanding how people think, I’m always eager to dive in.

After finishing my Bachelor’s in Commerce, I realized something important — numbers and spreadsheets didn’t make my heart race. I wanted to create, imagine, and design. So I took a leap and enrolled in a one-year Graphic Design and Animation course. That’s where my creative journey truly began. I learned how to bring ideas to life, play with visuals, and tell stories through design.

My first job as a Graphic Designer was exciting — I got to turn concepts into visuals every day. But soon, I found myself asking bigger questions: Why do people click certain buttons? What makes them stay on a page? I wasn’t just satisfied designing; I wanted to understand the why behind it. That’s when I discovered UX and UI design — the perfect balance between creativity and logic. I took up a six-month UX/UI design course, and everything suddenly made sense. It felt like I had found my true direction — designing experiences, not just interfaces.

Today, I see design as a beautiful mix of art, empathy, and problem-solving. It’s not just about making things look good — it’s about making them feel right for the people who use them. I’m constantly experimenting, learning, and evolving, because that’s what creativity is all about — endless curiosity with a dash of fun!
