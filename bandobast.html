What is Bandobast

The Bandobast App and Dashboard is a powerful digital tool that makes event security planning easier and more efficient. It helps manage everything from event permissions to live monitoring and post-event reports, making the whole process smarter and more organized

Overview

The Bandobast App and Dashboard is a smart digital tool designed to improve how events are planned and managed from a security perspective. It helps manage everything from getting event permissions to live monitoring and reporting after the event. This makes the whole process more organised, faster, and more efficient.

My Role

UX Design, UI Design

Tools

Timeline

May - June, 2024

Team

1 Lead, 1 designer, 1 developer

The Challenge

Every journey starts with a user’s struggle. I begin by asking: What’s causing friction? What’s missing? How can we turn frustration into delight? and these are the challenges i have received from the users.

Event security planning was done manually, making it slow, inefficient, and prone to errors.
Authorities had limited ability to track activities during events in real-time, leading to delayed responses in case of issues
Post-event analysis and reporting were scattered or inconsistent, making it hard to learn from past events or improve future planning.
Different teams working on the event (security, administration ) often lacked a centralized platform for coordination.
Important data related to permissions, security plans, or incidents could easily be lost or recorded inaccurately.
Obtaining event permissions involved lengthy paperwork and coordination between multiple departments.
Without digital logs, tracking who did what and when was difficult, reducing accountability.

The Solution

I listened, mapped the mess, and designed with empathy—turning pain points into smooth paths and confusion into delight

Brings all police coordination tasks onto one platform
Created room for communication between different departments
Allowed to Manages manpower and resource allocation efficiently within the app.
Provided documentation and record-keeping feature
Offers detailed post-event reports
Provides live updates during events feature.

Research

Initial Research

While working on the Bandobast app, I directly engaged with police personnel at different levels — from constables to commissioners — to understand their daily challenges, duties, and key requirements during Bandobast operations.

Defining the users:

Before starting the design, we focused on clearly identifying the different types of users involved in Bandobast operations. Understanding their roles helped us map their specific needs and design relevant solutions for each group.

We identified three key user groups:

Constables & Head Constables:

Ground-Level Officers

Focus: Duty clarity, location updates, easy check-ins

SI / Inspectors

Supervisory Officers

Focus: Team assignments, Co-Ordination, Incident Reporting

ACP / DCP / Commissioners

Senior Officers

Focus: Strategic overview, resource allocation, real-time data

Interviews:

To better understand what features to prioritise in the Bandobast app, I conducted a series of discussions with police personnel across different ranks. The goal was to gather insights on what kind of structure and information layout would be most useful for their day-to-day operations.

Some of the questions i have asked with user group

For Constables & Head Constables:

  • How do you currently receive your Bandobast duties?
  • How do you report your attendance?
  • Have you ever missed any important instructions due to lack of communication?

For Inspectors and SIs:

  • How do you plan and assign for an event?
  • What tools do you currently use for communication and reporting?
  • Do you face difficulties in tracking team members during Bandobast? if yes, what kind of difficulties

For ACPs / Commissioners:

  • Do you get real-time reports from ground staff?
  • How do you report your attendance?
  • Have you ever missed any important instructions due to lack of communication?

A few Pain points:

  • Officers receive last-minute or unclear instructions.
  • Printed lists or verbal communication lead to confusion.
  • Inability to quickly inform all deployed staff in emergencies.
  • Junior officers often miss crucial updates.
  • Ground-level officers struggle to report issues quickly to supervisors.
  • Difficult to track past deployments or analyze duty performance data.

Wish lists:

  • View duty location, time, and responsibilities clearly in-app.
  • Immediate alerts for any changes or emergencies.
  • Broadcast messaging from senior officers.
  • Visual overview of who is deployed where.
  • Helps manage staff during large-scale events.

Competitor Analysis:

Since most police departments still rely on manual processes, direct competitors are rare. but there are, partial systems that help us learn what works and what doesn't.

Apps:

  • Hawk Eye, Whats-app

Strengths

  • Track beat assignments
  • GPS-based officer monitoring
  • Emergency alert buttons

Weakness

  • Not focused on large-scale event deployments
  • UI often outdated and not user-friendly

Home Dashboard ( Nodal Officer )

This screen showcases the Home Dashboard for the Nodal Officer. From here, the officer can create new bandobast events based on requests received from various event organisers (government, public, or private).The interface is designed to offer a quick snapshot of key upcoming events, categorised by type and priority, helping officers plan and allocate resources efficiently.

Event Creation

This is the dedicated Event Creation page used by a Nodal Officer to register a new Bandobast event, based on a request from an event organizer. The form allows officers to input essential event details like name, type, schedule, location, and urgency level. It also provides space to specify which departments need to be involved and submit related documents.

Home Dashboard ( Inspector)

Once an event is created and submitted by the Nodal Officer, it is automatically routed to the assigned Inspector for operational planning.

Allocating Forces

In Second screen, allows the inspector to:

  • View the event name, type, venue, date, time, and priority level.
  • Select the number and type of officers needed, including:
    • Inspectors
    • Sub-Inspectors
    • Head Constables
    • Constables
  • Simply tap checkboxes to assign available personnel for the event.

At the bottom, the inspector can either Clear the form or Confirm & Submit the deployment plan.

Assign Duty

In the Bandobast app, once an event is created and reviewed, higher-ranking officers such as Inspectors or senior officers are responsible for assigning specific roles and responsibilities to the police force. This process ensures that every officer knows where to be, when to report, and what task to perform during the event.

Home Dashboard ( Constable)

The Constable homepage in the Bandobast app is designed to give on-ground officers a quick and clear overview of their assigned tasks and important real-time updates during an event.
This screen acts as the constable’s personal dashboard, helping them stay organised, informed, and ready for action.
