What is InPulse

InPulse is a personalised education platform designed to inspire growth by blending technology, real-time progress tracking, and life-skill development—empowering institutions to nurture future-ready, purpose-driven learners.

Overview

Think of the InPulse Dashboard as the campus’s friendly mastermind—making sure everyone’s on the same page. Sure, it saves faculty from drowning in paperwork, but its real magic is spotting each student’s superpowers. Then, it helps sharpen those powers so they can either land their dream job or go out and build an empire of their own.

The Challenge

Every journey starts with a struggle—and everyone here felt it. Teachers had no easy way to track students. HODs were stuck with too many files. Coordinators were tired of jumping between tools. Principals wanted one clear view but got bits and pieces. And students just wanted learning to make sense. So we asked—what’s going wrong, what’s missing, and how do we fix it? That’s where the real change began

One-size-fits-all curriculum fails to meet diverse student needs, interests, and learning speeds.
Many graduates lack the practical skills and industry readiness expected by employers.
Focus remains heavily on technical knowledge, with minimal attention to soft skills, adaptability, and collaboration.
Students often leave due to lack of direction, support, or motivation.
Teachers juggle teaching, mentoring, admin tasks, and reporting—often without digital support.
Assessment methods focus more on memory than practical application or innovation.
Collaboration among students is underutilized, missing opportunities for peer mentorship and collective growth.

The Solution

WI listened, mapped the mess, and designed with empathy—turning pain points into smooth paths and confusion into delight

InPulse adapts learning experiences based on student performance, interests, and pace—ensuring no learner is left behind.
Integrates real-world projects, internships, and certifications aligned with current industry needs to bridge the skill gap.
Simplifies scheduling, attendance, mentoring, and reporting—empowering faculty to focus on impactful teaching.
Centralizes all institutional data—from academics to placement —into one intelligent, integrated platform.
Offers dashboards and analytics for students, faculty, and administrators to monitor academic and non-academic growth dynamically.
Facilitates forums, team-based projects, and discussion spaces to nurture a culture of collaborative learning.

Research

Initial Research

While building the InPulse web app, I went on a campus-wide adventure—chatting with everyone from instructors, HODs, and coordinators to the principal, dean, and even the admissions, finance, and placement teams. I also sat down with students to hear about their day-to-day wins and woes. Each conversation revealed little secrets—how people work, where they struggle, and what would make their lives easier. These stories became my treasure map, guiding me to design a web app that feels natural for everyone, whether you’re an admin juggling approvals or a student chasing deadlines.

Defining the users:

Before starting the design for the InPulse web app, we focused on clearly identifying the different types of users across the institution. Understanding the roles of instructors, HODs, coordinators, department heads, and students helped us map their specific needs and design tailored solutions for each user group.

We identified three key user groups:

Academic & Administrative Staff

Roles: Instructors, HODs, Coordinators, Module Coordinators, Principal, Dean

Focus: Course planning, performance tracking, approvals, academic reviews, and reporting

Support Departments

Roles: Admissions, Finance, Placement

Focus: Student onboarding, fee tracking, placement data - management, and departmental coordination

Students

Senior Officers

Focus: Access to learning resources, progress tracking, feedback, mentoring, and skill development

Interviews:

When we started building the InPulse web app, I didn’t just sit behind a desk—I went straight to the source. I sat with instructors between classes, caught HODs in their busy corridors, chatted with department heads over coffee, and listened to students in their favorite hangout spots. I wanted the unfiltered version of their day—what flows, what frustrates, and what could be better. Those stories, small struggles, and big ideas became the blueprint for a system that doesn’t just “work,” but feels like it was made for them—making academics smoother, admin lighter, and student journeys clearer.

Some of the questions i have asked with user group

For Students:

  • What tools or platforms do you currently use to track your academic progress?
  • Do you feel supported in your learning beyond academics (skills, career guidance, etc.)?
  • What kind of features would help you manage your learning more effectively?

For Instructors:

  • How do you currently manage your classes, attendance,
  • What challenges do you face while tracking student performance or progress?
  • What kind of reports or analytics would be helpful to you as a faculty member?

For HOD’s

  • How do you currently monitor faculty performance and class delivery?
  • Are there specific challenges in tracking student outcomes or mentoring effectiveness?
  • Is it easy to get a holistic view of departmental activities?

For Admission Department

  • How do you currently monitor faculty performance and class delivery?
  • How do you communicate updates or missing information to applicants?
  • Are there any repetitive tasks you wish could be automated?

For Placement Department

  • How do you collect and manage student placement data (profiles, resumes, etc.)?
  • How do you track student eligibility and participation in placements?
  • What kind of analytics or insights would help you better prepare students for placements?

A few Pain points:

  • Absence of skill tracking beyond academics (life skills, certifications, etc.).
  • Lack of clarity on academic progress and outcomes.
  • Time-consuming report generation and documentation for HODs
  • Difficulty in tracking student progress in a structured, real-time manner.
  • No easy way to monitor ongoing faculty activity or student outcomes.
  • Difficulty matching students to company criteria due to lack of filtered data.

Wish lists:

  • A personal dashboard showing course, skills, and placement readiness
  • Intuitive tools to take attendance, assign grades, and monitor mentoring
  • Central dashboard showing department metrics
  • Searchable student database with filters (skills, marks, interests)

Instructor

CO - PO Mapping at a Click

Earlier, faculty spent weeks buried in paperwork—scribbling course outcomes, cross-checking with program outcomes, and preparing endless tables for accreditation. With InPulse’s CO-PO Mapping, all of that happens on a single screen. Faculty can directly link COs with POs, align competencies and indicators, and generate structured mappings in minutes. This cuts down 90% of paperwork, reduces errors, and frees them to focus on teaching instead of tables. InPulse didn’t just digitize paperwork; it reimagined academic planning into a smarter, faster, and stress-free process.

From Chalkboard Notes to Smart Lesson Plans

Traditionally, faculty spent hours scribbling lesson plans, mapping outcomes, and filling endless paperwork just to meet academic requirements. With the Lesson Plan Builder, all of that happens in one place—modules, outcomes, Bloom’s levels, delivery modes, timelines, and references can be created, tracked, and exported with a few clicks. What used to take weeks of manual work is now automated and transparent. freeing them to focus on what truly matters—teaching and mentoring students.

HOD

HOD Scheme Builder — the blueprint that powers everything

Every semester in InPulse starts at the HOD’s desk. With the Scheme Builder, the HOD lays out the full academic blueprint—NBA course codes, categories, titles, teaching hours (theory/tutorial/practical/SDA), exam pattern (duration, CIE/SEE marks), and credits—right in one clean table. The moment it’s saved, that scheme becomes the single source of truth: coordinators and instructors are auto-notified, their next steps unlock, and downstream screens (lesson plans, CO-PO mapping, timetables, reports) are prefilled and consistent. No more Excel ping-pong, duplicate entries, or version confusion—just role-based control, audit-ready accuracy, and a department that moves in sync.

From Chalkboard Notes to Smart Lesson Plans

InPulse transforms faculty assignment into a one-click process—replacing hours of spreadsheets and emails with a clear, unified view. HODs assign coordinators and instructors in minutes, ensuring accuracy, accountability, and a smooth semester start.

Students

Skill Builder: Courses Beyond the Classroom

Skill Builder Courses allow students to register for subjects of their interest. Once enrolled, they receive tailored recommendations for events, design challenges, projects, and more. This feature not only nurtures curiosity but also helps students enhance their talents and build practical skills beyond academics

Bridging Skill Gaps for Career Readiness

The KSM (Knowledge, Skill, Mind) framework helps students understand their thinking style, skills, and knowledge levels from the very first year. Faculty provide assessments through the platform, and based on the results, students see where they stand and what gaps they need to work on. The platform then recommends personalized courses to bridge those gaps, ensuring steady growth throughout their academic journey.

  • Assessments: Knowledge, Skill, and Mind tests conducted by faculty.
  • Gap Analysis: Compares current level with desired skills/career goals.
  • Personalized Learning: Suggests relevant courses and activities.
  • Continuous Growth: From 1st to 4th year, preparing students to make the right career decisions.
